The National Challenge

Rwanda builds fast.
Intelligence must match ambition.

NST2 represents Rwanda's most ambitious transformation programme. Yet the systems governing it remain fragmented, reactive, and incapable of seeing systemic risk before it materialises.

01
Fragmented Decision Architecture

33 outcome indicators across 5 pillars managed through disconnected ministry systems. Critical interdependencies remain invisible until they become governance failures.

02
No Predictive Capability

Infrastructure failures, demand surges, and fiscal overruns are discovered after they occur. Rwanda's development targets require foresight that reactive M&E systems cannot provide.

03
Unmodelled Investment Risk

FRW 63,559B in NST2 capital flows without simulation-backed validation. Development banks and sponsors lack the tools to model return across interconnected national systems.

04
No System-Level Oversight

Airports, energy grids, logistics corridors, agriculture, and smart cities all interact. Without a national intelligence platform, planners cannot see how one sector cascades across others.
